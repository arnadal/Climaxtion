What is Climate Change?

Climate Change is an influence in the average weather conditions and patterns (climate) of an area over a long period of time. It can harm crops cultivated in an area, clothes hung in an area for drying, food present in the region, etc. Climate Change is slowly but surely affecting our earth due to human activites such as harmful gases released from factories which are heating the atmosphere and therefore, we have made Climaxtion to spread awareness and take action against it.



— Jayden Haokip Chief Researcher

Climate change refers to the long-driven shifts in temperatures and weather patterns in the atmosphere these may be natural but since the Industrial Revolution, human activities in factories have been a major issue too. Mainly due to the burning of coal, oil, and gas. Burning fossil fuels generates greenhouse gas emissions like a blanket wrapped around the Earth, trapping the sun’s heat and raising temperatures. Climate change threatens people with food and water scarcity, increased flooding, extreme heat, disease, and economic loss. Human migration and conflict can also be a result. The World Health Organization (WHO) calls climate change the greatest threat to global health in the 21st century.



— Swastik Rawat Chief Editor
